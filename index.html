About Us

The Government of India's first space exploration organisation, with its headquarters in Bengaluru, is known as the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO). In order to develop and use space technology for national development, while also pursuing planetary exploration and space science research, ISRO was established in 1969.

Over the years, ISRO has established a reputation as one of the most innovative and successful space organisations in the world by effectively showcasing its cutting-edge technologies. Aryabhata, the first Indian satellite, was created by the ISRO and launched on April 19, 1975, with assistance from the Soviet Union.

The first satellite to be successfully launched by the Indian-made SLV-3 launch vehicle was Rohini, which was launched in 1980. Later, with further effort, ISRO created two additional rockets: the GSLV (Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle), which is used to launch satellites into geostationary orbits, and the PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle), which is used to launch satellites into polar orbits. Both rockets have successfully launched a number of communication and earth observation satellites for India.

Mangalyam

454 crore

The Mars Orbiter Mission(MOM) or Mangalyaan, is a space probe launched by the ISRO on November 5, 2013. Mangalyaan was India's first interplanetary missioand it's been in the Martian orbit since September 24, 2014.They have also made a hit Tamil movie which was released on 29 July, 2022.

Chandrayaan-1

Rs.386 crore

On October 22, 2008, an Indian PSLV rocket launched the Chandrayaan-1 spacecraft into Earth orbit. After a series of orbit-raising maneuvers, Chandrayaan-1 successfully entered orbit around the Moon on November 8 of that year.the mission achieved most of its scientific objectives including detecting presence of Lunar Water.

Astrosat

Rs.220 crore

Astrosat, a short form of Astronomy satellite.AstroSat is the first dedicated Indian astronomy mission aimed at studying celestial sources in X-ray, optical and UV spectral bands simultaneously. India's first dedicated multi-wavelength observatory mission has completed seven years in orbit in September 2022.

Gangayan

Rs 9,023 crores

-India’s first manned mission to space. ISRO will first launch two unmanned missions into space ahead of the manned trip. The launch will happen in the end 2023. The purpose of deploying uncrewed missions is to demonstrate and test the technology, safety, and reliability of systems before the crewed flight.

Aditya-L1

Rs 378.53 crore

This is the first Indian mission to study the Sun. Let’s first decode the meaning of Aditya – L1. A satellite will be placed in the halo orbit around the Lagrangian point 1 because that way the satellite can continuously study the Sun without any eclipses.The Aditya L1 will launch in the first quarter of 2023.

Chandrayaan-3

Rs 615 crores

Chandrayaan-3 is ISRO’s third lunar exploration mission, the objective of which is soft-landing on the Moon.The mission will likely use a repeat of the parts from Chandrayaan-2 mission.It will not have an orbiter, but its propulsion module will behave like a communications relay satellite.

ISRO'S ICONS

Dr. Vikram Sarabhai

1963 to 1971

He is considered the Father of the Indian space program.He established Physical Research Laboratory, Indian Institute of Management Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre and Faster Breeder Test Reactor

Prof. Udupi Ramachandra Rao

1984-1994

In 1972, Dr. Rao took on the job of establishing satellite technology in India. He played a key part in the 1975 design of the first Indian satellite, Aryabhata.

Dr. Krishnaswamy Kasturirangan

1994-2003

Under his direction as ISRO Chairman, PSLV and GSLV have achieved numerous milestones for ISRO. Additionally, he oversaw the development of India's first two experimental BHASKARA-I and II earth observation satellites.
